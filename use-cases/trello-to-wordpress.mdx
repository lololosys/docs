# Automate Your Content Workflow with Docswrite

Docswrite makes it easy to automate your entire content workflow—from drafting in Google Docs to publishing on your website or CMS. Eliminate manual copy-paste, streamline collaboration, and ensure your content is always formatted and published exactly how you want.

## Why Automate Your Content Workflow?

Manual content processes are slow, error-prone, and hard to scale. Automation helps you:

- Save time by reducing repetitive tasks
- Maintain consistent formatting and branding
- Collaborate seamlessly with your team in Google Docs
- Publish to multiple platforms with a single click
- Scale your content production without extra effort

## How Docswrite Automates Your Workflow

With Docswrite, you can:

- **Connect your CMS** (WordPress, Contentful, etc.) to Docswrite
- **Link your Google Account** to access and manage your docs
- **Draft and collaborate** in Google Docs as usual
- **Export and publish** directly from Google Docs to your website
- **Bulk export** multiple docs at once for large-scale publishing
- **Map custom fields** and SEO metadata automatically

## Step-by-Step: Automating Your Content Workflow

### 1. Connect Your CMS

1. Go to the [Docswrite Dashboard](https://docswrite.com/dashboard).
2. Click **Connect Site** and select your CMS (e.g., WordPress).
3. Follow the prompts to authenticate and set up the connection.

> For more details, see [WordPress Connection Guide](/essentials/wordpress-connection).

### 2. Link Your Google Account

1. In the dashboard, click **Connect Google Account**.
2. Authorize Docswrite to access your Google Docs.

### 3. Draft and Collaborate in Google Docs

- Create your content in Google Docs as you normally would.
- Collaborate with your team, leave comments, and make edits in real time.

### 4. Export and Publish

1. In Docswrite, select the Google Doc(s) you want to publish.
2. Click **Export** and choose your connected CMS.
3. Configure export options (post type, categories, tags, SEO fields, custom fields).
4. Click **Publish** to send your content live.

Docswrite will automatically convert your Google Doc into a published post or page, preserving formatting, images, and metadata.

### 5. Bulk Export (Optional)

- Select multiple Google Docs to export and publish in bulk—perfect for content migrations or large campaigns.

## Supported Features

- **Formatting**: Headings, lists, tables, images, and more
- **SEO**: Supports YoastSEO, RankMath, and ACF fields
- **Images**: Inline images are uploaded to your CMS media library
- **Custom Fields**: Map Google Doc content to custom fields in your CMS
- **Bulk Export**: Publish multiple docs at once
